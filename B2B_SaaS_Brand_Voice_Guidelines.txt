B2B SAAS BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES
TechFlow Analytics - Updated March 2026

COMPANY MISSION
Help marketing teams make better decisions faster through data-driven insights and intuitive analytics.

OUR BRAND VOICE
Professional but approachable. Data-driven but human. Confident but never arrogant.

CORE VOICE PRINCIPLES

1. CLEAR VALUE PROPOSITION
B2B buyers are busy. Lead with benefits, back with proof, make the value immediately obvious.

Guidelines:
- Start with outcomes: "Reduce reporting time by 50%" not "Our platform has many features"
- Use specific metrics and numbers
- Show ROI clearly and quickly
- Avoid vague promises: Replace "improve performance" with "increase conversion rates by 23%"
- Format: Problem → Solution → Proof
- Example: "Spending 10 hours on weekly reports? Automate them in 10 minutes. Join 500+ marketing teams saving 20 hours per week."

2. PROFESSIONALLY CONFIDENT
We know our product works. We've proven it. We're confident without being cocky.

Guidelines:
- Use data and case studies, not hype
- "Our customers see 40% faster time-to-insight" not "We're revolutionizing the industry"
- Avoid superlatives: best, greatest, most innovative
- Use customer proof: "Used by 500+ teams at companies like Spotify, Airbnb, Stripe"
- Be direct: "This will save you time" not "This might help you work more efficiently"
- Show, don't tell: Replace "easy to use" with "Get insights in 3 clicks"

3. TECHNICALLY CREDIBLE
We speak to smart people who understand marketing and data. We don't dumb it down, but we don't overcomplicate either.

Guidelines:
- Use industry terminology correctly: attribution, CAC, LTV, funnel analysis
- Explain new concepts simply: "Multi-touch attribution = see which channels actually drive conversions"
- Balance technical depth with accessibility
- Assume baseline knowledge; don't patronize
- Provide depth in resources, clarity in marketing
- Example: "Track full-funnel attribution across 12+ channels with our unified data model" not "See where your customers come from"

4. ACTION-ORIENTED
Every piece of content should drive toward a clear next step. No dead ends.

Guidelines:
- Strong, clear CTAs: "Start free trial" not "Learn more"
- Show the path forward: "Book a demo → See your data → Start tracking in 48 hours"
- Use active verbs: Track, optimize, analyze, improve, scale
- Make next steps obvious and easy
- Remove friction in language: "No credit card required" "14-day free trial" "Cancel anytime"
- Example: "Try TechFlow free for 14 days. No credit card. Cancel anytime." not "Interested in learning about our solutions?"

TONE BY CONTENT TYPE

Website Landing Pages:
- Scannable with clear hierarchy
- Lead with strongest benefit
- Use bullet points for features
- Social proof above the fold
- Clear, prominent CTA

Blog Posts:
- Educational and helpful
- Data-driven insights
- Practical takeaways
- Industry trends and analysis
- Example: "5 Attribution Models Compared: Which Drives Better ROI?"

Email Campaigns:
- Subject lines: Specific benefit or data point
- Personalized where possible
- Scannable (bullets, short paragraphs)
- Single clear CTA
- Example subject: "Your Q1 marketing data is ready" not "Check out our latest insights"

Social Media (LinkedIn):
- Professional tone
- Lead with insight or data
- Encourage discussion
- Share customer wins
- Example: "40% of marketers still use last-click attribution. Here's why that's costing you conversions 👇"

Product Copy:
- Feature → Benefit format
- Technical accuracy required
- Include use cases
- Show integrations
- Example: "Slack integration: Get daily performance alerts where your team already works"

LANGUAGE TO USE

POWER VERBS:
✓ Track, measure, analyze, optimize
✓ Scale, automate, streamline
✓ Increase, improve, reduce
✓ Identify, discover, unlock
✓ Convert, engage, retain

DATA LANGUAGE:
✓ "40% increase in qualified leads"
✓ "Save 20 hours per week"
✓ "Used by 500+ teams"
✓ "4.8/5 on G2"
✓ "$2M+ in attributed revenue"

AVOID:
✗ Leverage, utilize, synergy
✗ Revolutionary, game-changing, disruptive
✗ Solutions, ecosystem, paradigm shift
✗ World-class, best-in-class
✗ Cutting-edge, next-generation
✗ Empower (overused in SaaS)

GRAMMAR & STYLE
- Use contractions for approachability: "We're" "You'll" "Don't"
- Numbers: Use numerals (10+, 40%, $2M)
- Percentages: Always with % symbol
- Lists: Parallel structure required
- Headlines: Sentence case, not title case
- Hyphens: Multi-touch, cross-channel, full-funnel

EXAMPLE REWRITES

BEFORE: "Our revolutionary analytics solution leverages cutting-edge AI technology to empower marketing teams to achieve unprecedented levels of performance optimization across all channels."

AFTER: "See which marketing channels drive revenue. Track ROI across all campaigns in one dashboard. Make better budget decisions with data, not guesses."

BEFORE: "TechFlow offers a comprehensive suite of analytics capabilities designed to meet the diverse needs of modern marketing organizations."

AFTER: "Track attribution, analyze funnels, and report on ROI—all in one platform. Built for marketing teams at companies like yours."

BEFORE: "Interested in learning more about how our platform might be able to help your team?"

AFTER: "See TechFlow in action. Book a 15-minute demo and get your first insights tomorrow."

PROOF POINTS WE USE
- Customer count: "500+ marketing teams"
- Specific metrics: "40% faster time-to-insight"
- Time savings: "Save 20 hours per week"
- G2 rating: "4.8/5 on G2"
- Customer logos: Spotify, Airbnb, Stripe
- Integration count: "Connects to 50+ tools"

COMPETITIVE POSITIONING
DO:
✓ Show specific differentiators with data
✓ "Only platform with real-time multi-touch attribution"
✓ "2x faster than alternatives (verified by G2)"

DON'T:
✗ Name competitors directly
✗ Generic "better than" claims
✗ Negative positioning

VOICE CHECKLIST
Before publishing, ask:
□ Did I lead with a clear benefit?
□ Is there proof (data, testimonial, case study)?
□ Would a busy VP of Marketing immediately understand the value?
□ Is the CTA obvious and friction-free?
□ Did I remove buzzwords and jargon?
□ Is this specific enough to be credible?

Remember: B2B buyers are skeptical and busy. Respect their time. Prove your value. Make the next step obvious.
