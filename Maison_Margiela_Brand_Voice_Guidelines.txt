MAISON MARGIELA BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES
Internal Document - March 2026

MAISON PHILOSOPHY
Maison Martin Margiela is an avant-garde fashion house that deconstructs, reimagines, and challenges conventional notions of luxury. We are conceptual, intellectual, and deeply rooted in craft.

THE MARGIELA VOICE
Our voice is refined, enigmatic, and intentionally understated. We speak to those who understand that true luxury whispers.

CORE VOICE PRINCIPLES

1. INTELLECTUAL & CONCEPTUAL
We create fashion as art. Our communication should reflect depth of thought, creative process, and conceptual rigor.

Guidelines:
- Reference the creative process, not just the product
- Use architectural and artistic vocabulary when appropriate
- Celebrate craftsmanship and technique
- Explain the "why" behind design choices
- Favor thoughtful interpretation over obvious description
- Example: "Deconstructed tailoring reveals the architecture beneath" not "This jacket has visible seams"

2. UNDERSTATED ELEGANCE
Luxury does not shout. We are confident enough to be subtle, leaving room for discovery and personal interpretation.

Guidelines:
- Less is more—every word must earn its place
- Avoid superlatives and exaggeration
- Let the work speak through implication
- Use precise, specific language
- Favor "the collection explores" over "amazing new designs"
- Example: "A study in tension between structure and deconstruction" not "Stunning pieces you'll love"

3. ARTISANAL & MATERIAL-FOCUSED
We celebrate craft, material integrity, and the human hand. Our voice should honor technique and materiality.

Guidelines:
- Name materials specifically: "Italian calf leather" not "premium leather"
- Reference techniques: hand-stitched, deconstructed, reconstructed
- Acknowledge the makers: "Crafted in our atelier"
- Describe tactile qualities: weight, drape, texture
- Use technical fashion terminology correctly
- Example: "Hand-finished seams in Italian cotton poplin" not "Quality fabric with great attention to detail"

4. ENIGMATIC & OPEN TO INTERPRETATION
We embrace ambiguity. The Margiela aesthetic invites questions rather than providing all answers.

Guidelines:
- Leave space for interpretation
- Use evocative rather than prescriptive language  
- Pose questions to the reader when appropriate
- Avoid over-explanation
- Trust the audience's intelligence
- Example: "What remains when form is stripped to essence?" not "This minimalist design is perfect for modern living"

TONE BY MEDIUM

Runway Show Descriptions:
- Poetic but precise
- Reference artistic/architectural concepts
- Describe atmosphere and intention
- Technical details integrated seamlessly

Product Descriptions:
- Material-first approach
- Technique and construction details
- Measurements and specifications
- Minimal editorializing—let the piece speak

Social Media:
- Visual-led (image is primary)
- Minimal captions—often just collection name
- No emojis
- No direct calls-to-action
- Example: "Artisanal Line Autumn-Winter 2026"

Press Releases:
- Factual and detailed
- Collection themes and inspirations
- Craft and technique focus
- Avoid marketing language

GRAMMATICAL STYLE
- Favor present tense
- Use sentence fragments when impactful
- Longer, more complex sentences acceptable for depth
- Semicolons welcome
- Em dashes for emphasis
- Minimal use of exclamation points (almost never)

EXAMPLE REWRITES

BEFORE: "These amazing new boots are perfect for fall! Made with premium leather and featuring a unique deconstructed design. Limited quantities available—shop now!"

AFTER: "Deconstructed ankle boots in Italian calfskin. Hand-stitched construction reveals the footwear's architectural form. Autumn-Winter 2026 collection."

BEFORE: "Our iconic Tabi boot gets a modern update with this season's freshest silhouettes."

AFTER: "The Tabi—reimagined. Split-toe construction meets contemporary volumes in vegetable-tanned leather."

BEFORE: "Maison Margiela is known for pushing boundaries and creating innovative, cutting-edge fashion that challenges traditional luxury."

AFTER: "The Maison deconstructs conventional codes, revealing fashion as a conceptual practice."

BRAND CODES TO REFERENCE
- The four white stitches (never explain, just reference)
- Tabi boot (iconic, treat as known)
- Numbered collections (0-23)
- Anonymous creative direction (part of ethos)
- Blank label (intentional absence of branding)

WHAT WE ARE NOT
❌ Accessible or overtly commercial
❌ Trend-focused or seasonal
❌ Celebrity-driven or influencer-led
❌ Loud or ostentatious
❌ Explanatory to the point of obviousness

PHOTOGRAPHY & VISUAL DIRECTION (Context for Copy)
When writing for visual content, remember:
- Margiela aesthetic is raw, unpolished, intentional
- Studio lighting, often stark
- Focus on garment construction and detail
- Models as forms, not personalities
- White, neutral, or industrial backgrounds

VOICE CHECKLIST
Before publishing, ask:
□ Have I removed all marketing clichés?
□ Does this respect the reader's intelligence?
□ Is the materiality/craft evident?
□ Would this feel at home in an art catalog?
□ Have I been specific rather than generic?
□ Is there room for interpretation?

Remember: We do not chase approval. We create with conviction and communicate with the same integrity. The right audience will find us.
