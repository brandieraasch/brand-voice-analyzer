ALAN BRAND VOICE GUIDELINES
Last Updated: March 2026

BRAND MISSION
Help people live in good health to 100 while helping employers feel proud, turning health benefits from a cost centre into their most valuable investment.

OUR VOICE
Alan's voice is clear, helpful, and human. We make healthcare accessible by speaking plainly and caring deeply.

CORE VOICE PRINCIPLES

1. CLARITY ABOVE ALL
We explain complex healthcare topics in simple language. No jargon. No corporate speak. If your grandmother couldn't understand it, rewrite it.

Guidelines:
- Use short sentences (15 words max when possible)
- Break down complex topics into digestible chunks
- Define technical terms in plain language
- Favor "health insurance" over "complementary health coverage"
- Favor "doctor" over "healthcare practitioner"
- Use active voice: "We help you" not "You are helped by us"

2. GENUINELY HELPFUL
We're not selling insurance—we're helping people access better health. Every piece of content should answer: "How does this help the reader?"

Guidelines:
- Lead with benefits, not features
- Use "you" language (not "our members" or "customers")
- Anticipate questions and answer them
- Give actionable next steps
- Focus on prevention, not just treatment

3. WARMTH & EMPATHY
Healthcare is personal. We show we understand without being overly emotional or patronizing.

Guidelines:
- Acknowledge difficulties: "Navigating health insurance is confusing"
- Use encouraging language: "You've got this"
- Never talk down to people
- Show we're on their side: "We're here to help"
- Balance warmth with professionalism

4. TRANSPARENT & HONEST
We practice radical transparency. If something is complicated, we say so. If we don't know, we admit it.

Guidelines:
- Never hide important information in fine print
- Explain the "why" behind policies
- Admit when healthcare is complex: "This is confusing, here's why..."
- No false promises or overselling
- Be upfront about limitations

TONE VARIATIONS BY PLATFORM

Blog Posts:
- Conversational but informative
- Use storytelling where relevant
- Include practical examples
- Longer sentences OK if needed for clarity

Social Media:
- Punchy and accessible
- Use emojis sparingly (🌱 ✨ 💜)
- Questions to engage: "What's your wellness habit?"
- Always educational, never salesy

Emails:
- Personal and direct
- Subject lines: clear, not clever
- Scannable with bullets
- Clear call-to-action

Product Copy:
- Feature + benefit format
- Specific numbers and details
- Remove unnecessary words
- Focus on outcomes

WHAT WE ARE NOT
❌ Corporate or bureaucratic
❌ Patronizing or condescending  
❌ Overly casual or unprofessional
❌ Fear-mongering or anxiety-inducing
❌ Salesy or pushy

EXAMPLE REWRITES

BEFORE: "Alan provides comprehensive health insurance solutions with optimized coverage parameters and competitive pricing structures designed to maximize value while minimizing out-of-pocket expenditures."

AFTER: "We make health insurance simple. Get covered in 5 minutes, access care 24/7, and focus on prevention. Your health matters."

BEFORE: "Utilize our digital platform to schedule appointments with healthcare professionals."

AFTER: "Book a doctor's appointment in our app."

BEFORE: "Alan's preventive care initiatives have demonstrated significant improvements in member health outcomes."

AFTER: "Prevention works. Our members who use preventive care stay healthier—and it shows in the data."

KEY PHRASES WE USE
✅ "Your health matters"
✅ "We're here to help"
✅ "Prevention first"
✅ "Simple and clear"
✅ "Live healthier"

KEY PHRASES WE AVOID
❌ "Industry-leading solutions"
❌ "Comprehensive coverage parameters"
❌ "Optimal health outcomes"
❌ "Leverage our platform"
❌ "Best-in-class"

GRAMMAR & STYLE
- Use contractions: "We're" not "We are"
- Oxford comma: Yes
- Numbers: Spell out one-nine, use numerals 10+
- Dates: March 15, 2026
- Percent: 40% (no "percent")

ACCESSIBILITY GUIDELINES
- Reading level: 8th grade maximum
- Hemingway Editor score: 8 or below
- Avoid idioms that don't translate
- Consider non-native French speakers
- Screen reader friendly formatting

VOICE CHECKLIST
Before publishing, ask:
□ Is this clear to someone with no healthcare knowledge?
□ Does this genuinely help the reader?
□ Would I say this to a friend?
□ Is this transparent and honest?
□ Did I remove unnecessary jargon?

Remember: We're not just writing copy. We're helping people live healthier lives. Every word should serve that mission.
